Gun Safes & Safe Storage — What SA Law Requires

Gear Guide June 2026 4 min read

A proper safe isn't a nice-to-have — under the Firearms Control Act it's a legal requirement for every firearm owner in South Africa, and it's inspected before your licence is granted. Here's what you need to know, and how to choose a safe you won't outgrow.

What the law requires

The FCA regulations require that a firearm not under your direct physical control be stored in a prescribed, SABS-approved safe (built to the relevant SANS standard for gun safes), properly installed. In practice that means:

  • A safe carrying the correct SABS/SANS approval — not a filing cabinet with a lock.
  • Securely fixed to the structure of the building (bolted to a wall and/or floor).
  • Keys or codes under your control only — handing your safe key to an unlicensed person is an offence.

When you apply for a licence, the DFO can inspect your storage. No compliant safe, no licence — it's that direct. (Getting your paperwork started? Read our licence guide.)

Choosing the right size — buy bigger than you think

The most common regret we hear: "I should have bought the bigger one." Rifles with mounted scopes take more space than you expect, and most owners add a firearm (or three) over the years. Our rule of thumb: whatever you own now, buy at least two spaces more.

  • Handgun safes — compact wall or shelf-mounted units for a single pistol; from around R1,000.
  • 3–5 rifle safes — the standard home setup, with an internal lockable ammo compartment.
  • 6–16 rifle safes — for collectors and dedicated hunters; sliding-rack interiors make daily access easier.

Installation matters as much as the safe

Bolt through the back into brick or through the floor into concrete, using proper anchor bolts — not wood screws into a skirting board. Position the safe out of sight of windows and casual visitors. In coastal or damp areas, keep it off exterior walls where condensation forms.

Protect what's inside

A sealed steel box traps moisture, and rust never sleeps. Throw a dehumidifier unit in the safe (we stock the RAM Drisafe moisture absorber for under R100 — cheap insurance for a R30,000 rifle), wipe firearms down with an oiled cloth before they go in, and store ammunition in its own compartment, cool and dry.
