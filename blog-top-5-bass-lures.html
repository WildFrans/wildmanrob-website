Top 5 Lures for Bass Fishing in the Breede River Valley

Fishing March 2026 4 min read

Largemouth bass thrive in the farm dams and slower stretches of the Breede Valley, and you don't need a boat-load of tackle to catch them. These five lures — all available in our fishing department — cover practically every situation the valley throws at you.

1. Soft plastic worm (Texas-rigged)

If we could only sell you one bass lure, this is it. A 6-inch soft plastic worm in watermelon or junebug, Texas-rigged weedless, catches bass in every season. Cast to structure, let it sink, then crawl it back painfully slowly with small hops. Most strikes come on the drop — watch your line.

2. Spinnerbait

The searcher. When you don't know where the fish are, a 3/8 oz white or chartreuse spinnerbait lets you cover water fast. It bumps through reeds and sunken branches without snagging, and the flash-and-thump triggers reaction strikes even from bass that aren't feeding. Slow-roll it deeper in cold water; burn it shallow in summer.

3. Shallow crankbait

A square-bill crankbait in shad or craw colours is deadly along dam walls and rocky edges — exactly the structure our valley dams are full of. The trick most anglers miss: deliberately bounce it off rocks and wood. That erratic deflection is what converts followers into eaters.

4. Topwater popper (summer mornings)

Nothing in freshwater fishing beats a bass smashing a surface lure at dawn. From November through March, work a popper or walking bait around drowned trees and weed edges early and late in the day. Pop… pause… pop-pop… pause. Wait until you feel the fish before striking — the explosion comes before the hook-up.

5. Jig and craw trailer

The big-fish bait. A 1/4–3/8 oz jig with a craw trailer, dragged and hopped along the bottom near deep structure, targets the largest, laziest bass in the dam — especially in winter when everything else slows down. It takes patience, but the valley's trophy fish fall to jigs more than anything else.

Local colour rules of thumb

  • Clear water: natural colours — watermelon, green pumpkin, shad.
  • Stained water (after rain): darker and louder — junebug, black-and-blue, chartreuse.
  • Bright midday: go deeper and more natural; low light: darker silhouettes work.
